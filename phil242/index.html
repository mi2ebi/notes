what is philosophy?

philosophy comes from two greek words

  • φῐ́λος(phĭ́los) 'beloved, friendly'
  • σοφῐ́ᾱ(sophĭ́ā) 'wisdom'

what's wisdom? necessary conditions

  • get clear on what you believe
  • get clear on why you believe it
  • ask if the reasons justify the belief

what's justification? at minimum, would any rational, unbiased person agree that the reasons given are at least relevant to the belief?

metaphysics

(a) what do people have trouble determining the existence of?

  • quarks

according to judeochristian tradition, god is omnipotent (all-powerful), omniscient (all-knowing), omnibenevolent (all-good), and "eternal". theologians actually define eternal as "existing outside space and time"

what about cats vs people? people are also animate. how do people differ from other animals?

epistemology p1

epistemology
the study of knowledge (...hm)

epistemologists try to answer:

  • what can/do we know?
  • what can/do we know with certainty?
    (ie without the possibility of any doubt)

it's also useful to distinguish in principle vs in practice. in principle, we could know the soil composition of any planet in the universe. in practice we can't though, because our telescopes etc aren't that good

edge cases: can we know, in principle, infinity? human consciousness?

see also cognitive closure . can dogs ever know what π is?

metaphysics and epistemology

observations:

  1. we can think of possibilities beyond what we can know (eg the afterlife)
  2. it makes sense to say that there can exist things we can never know about, both in principle and in practice

knowledge

  1. can/do you know that 2 + 2 = 4?
    yes
  2. can/do you know you're reading my notes right now?
    yes
  3. do you know that 7 × 7 = 946?
    no
  4. do you know your phone is a lizard?
    no

claims 1 and 2 are true, and claims 3 and 4 are false.

knowledge is a kind of achievement: you can't know false things.

  1. do you know ผมเป็นครู? it means "i am a teacher"
    no – what are you lacking?
  2. what if i tell you more about it?
    • it's pronounced pom bpen kruu
    • the pom part, ผม, means "i"
    • the bpen part, เป็น, means "am"
    • the kruu part, ครู, means "teacher"
    • thai doesn't have a definite article
    • this letter → ค ← is the k
    • the letter after that is the r, and the uu is the squiggly thing under it
    now do you know it?
    no

would you feel comfortable explaining ผมเป็นครู to someone? no. what you're lacking is justification!

epistemology p2

the first meditation: on what can be called into doubt

¶1. descartes is demolishing his existing beliefs so he can have a stable foundation for new ones, both for his own benefit and for the benefit of philosophy as what-was-then-considered-a-science.

¶2. examining each individual belief would take literally forever. so we can just find some reason to doubt each of them. here again he can go directly to the foundation

¶3. namely, his senses

nb: descartes likes playing devils advocate with himself

argument from ordinary sense deception
(p1) it is unwise to trust anything that has decieved us even once
(p2) our senses sometimes decieve us
(c) we can never trust our senses

this argument fails because cases of sense deception are eminently correctable

¶4. but some beliefs that come from his senses are impossible to doubt

¶5. he also can't doubt that eg his hands are his; if he did he would be insane, right?

well, per the definition of wisdom,

  • get clear on what you believe
    i am certain my hand exists
  • get clear on why you believe it
    i can see, feel, ..., sense my hand

¶6-7. there isn't a reliable way to tell if you're dreaming or not. in a dream, if someone asked you "hey does your hand exist", dream!you would still say yes

dream argument
(p) there is no clear and certain way to distinguish dreams from reality
(c) everything we take to be reality might be a giant dream

ontology under the dream argument:

  • what would normally be in our everyday ontology, but is now cast into doubt?

well that'd be everything
