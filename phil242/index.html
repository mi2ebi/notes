what is philosophy?

philosophy comes from two greek words

  • φῐ́λος(phĭ́los) 'beloved, friendly'
  • σοφῐ́ᾱ(sophĭ́ā) 'wisdom'

what's wisdom? necessary conditions

  • get clear on what you believe
  • get clear on why you believe it
  • ask if the reasons justify the belief

what's justification? at minimum, would any rational, unbiased person agree that the reasons given are at least relevant to the belief?

metaphysics

metaphysics
the study of (a) what exists and (b) the way or manner in which it exists

(a) what do people have trouble determining the existence of?

  • quarks
  • redness
  • love
  • god

(b) if eg god does exist, what properties does he have?

according to judeochristian tradition, god is omnipotent (all-powerful), omniscient (all-knowing), omnibenevolent (all-good), and "eternal". theologians actually define eternal as "existing outside space and time"

ontology
the list of entities one claims exist

sample ontology:

  • rocks
  • cats
  • people
  • the number 1
  • loopholes in the law

how are eg rocks and cats similar? for one they're both tangible, or we could call them

matieral items
capable of physical subdivision

how rocks and cats differ? rocks and cats can both move, but cats can move volitionally (making them animate) and rocks can't (because they're inanimate)

what about cats vs people? people are also animate. how do people differ from other animals?

we're not alone in eg our usage of tools - some other animals can use tools for example

we're also not the only social animals, hm...

the classic dividing line is that we can acquire and use languages. other animals can communicate but that doesn't necessarily mean they have a language. for example bird calls aren't a language because they don't have enough variation in meaning. but maybe eg dolphin sounds are, we don't know. if it is a language, maybe dolphins in different parts of the ocean have different 'accents'

see also: kanzi

what about the number 1? unlike rocks and cats and people, the number 1 isn't a material item! it's abstract. does the number 1 exist?

would numbers exist if people never existed? a realist woud say yes. an anti-realist / pragmatist would say no.

would loopholes in the law exist if people never existed? i think not, because laws are made by people. people are necessary for "loopholes in the law" to exist.

some mathematicians claim to "invent" math, and others claim to "discover" it. if they discovered it, it must have already existed. we discovered mars but mars existed before people
